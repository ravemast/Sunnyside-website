How It Started

A Kettle, A Cart, and a Corner That Needed Shade

In 2025, our founder set up a single kettle and three stools on a street corner that everyone walked past but no one stopped at — until the chai started simmering. Within a month, the corner had regulars. Within a year, it had a name.

The recipes haven't changed much since. The cardamom still comes whole, the milk still gets watched closely, and the chai still gets poured with that satisfying high-arm stretch that cools it just enough to drink.

The Long Brew

Five Decades, Marked in Cups

2025

The First Kettle

A single cart, three stools, and a chai recipe scribbled on the back of a cigarette box.

2025

First Indoor Counter

The cart moved indoors to a small storefront — same stools, now with a roof and a record player.

2026

The Roastery Opens

We started roasting our own coffee in small batches, finally putting "& Coffee" in the name for good.

Today

Same Counter, New Faces

Three generations of regulars later, we're still pouring strong and slow — just with better chairs.

What We Stand For

The House Rules

Rule One

Slow Brewed, Always

No shortcuts on the kettle. Tea gets simmered, coffee gets filtered, and nothing leaves the counter before it's ready.

Rule Two

Local First

Our spices, milk, and bread come from vendors we've known for years — most of them longer than we've known our regulars.

Rule Three

Vinyl & Vibes

The record player has been running since 1989. If it's quiet in here, something's wrong.
